# What SCT does *not* explain — scope boundaries and load-bearing assumptions

Working note. The question driving this: *assuming the framework is correct in
everything it claims, what still doesn't fit, and where is "assuming it's right"
doing the heavy lifting?* Three categories: (A) your two specific intuitions,
sharpened; (B) conundrums that are simply out of scope even if SCT is right; (C)
internal claims where "right" is carrying more weight than the prose admits.

## A. Your two intuitions, sharpened

### Quantum gravity (if it even exists)

Correct that SCT sidesteps it rather than supplying it. The framework treats
Einstein-Cartan **classically** and argues QG is subdominant at
$\rho_C \sim 10^{50}$ kg/m³ (still 46 orders below Planck density). Three caveats:

- **The bounce is classical, but its trigger is quantum.** A1 needs quantum
  vacuum fluctuations to seed OG bounces; A3 evolves the bounce classically.
  The seam between them is *semiclassical gravity* — precisely the regime where
  we have no controlled theory. SCT lives on that seam and assumes it's benign.
- **"If it even exists" cuts both ways.** If gravity is fundamentally classical
  or emergent (no graviton, no spacetime quantization), SCT's classical EC
  bounce is friendlier — but then the quantum vacuum sourcing fluctuations on a
  classical background needs its own account.
- **Trans-Planckian problem.** Tier-2 perturbation modes (Bunch-Davies deep in
  collapse) get blueshifted through the bounce and hit trans-Planckian
  frequencies. Every bounce cosmology inherits this; SCT does too. Whether the
  predicted spectrum is sensitive to unknown short-distance (QG) physics is open
  — and it's exactly the calculation (Tier 2 / Q16) the framework stakes itself on.

### Elementary particles (your guess: "only really included through Cartasis")

Half right. The bounce *dismantles* particles to conserved currents
(energy-momentum, spin, chiral charge, B, L) and rebuilds Standard Model content
on the far side — so Cartasis is where particle identity is processed. But SCT
says nothing about **why those particles exist or have the properties they do**:

- the gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1), three generations, the Yukawa hierarchy,
  neutrino masses/mixing, the Higgs sector and its hierarchy problem — all
  **inputs**, inherited unchanged.
- Crucially, SCT assumes the **same Standard Model on both sides of every
  bounce** (it rebuilds from conserved currents using the same Lagrangian). That
  has a sharp consequence below (see Smolin tension in C).

## B. Out of scope even if SCT is entirely correct

1. **Where the laws come from.** SCT explains the *distribution of universe
   masses/sizes/chirality*, not the *values of the ~20+ Standard Model
   parameters* or the form of the Lagrangian. It relocates "initial conditions"
   up to the supraverse and inherits the laws as given. The deepest "why these
   laws" question is untouched.

2. **The cosmological constant problem — arguably worsened.** A3 still carries a
   bare $\Lambda$ ($R-2\Lambda$). SCT reattributes the *observed* acceleration to
   parent accretion (Ch. 6), but it does not explain why the QFT vacuum's
   zero-point energy (naively ~$10^{120}$ too large) fails to curve everything.
   An *infinite, eternally active* quantum vacuum (A1 + infinity) makes this more
   acute, not less: why is the supraverse substrate nearly flat rather than
   de Sitter at the cutoff scale? This is the single biggest physics problem the
   framework neither solves nor addresses.

3. **The measure problem / Boltzmann brains.** Ch. 6 dismisses Boltzmann's
   fluctuation idea, but an infinite equilibrium vacuum that occasionally
   fluctuates to $\rho_C$ *is* a Boltzmann scenario at the supraverse level. To
   make "we are typical observers near the population peak" (Ch. 4, Q25, the
   fine-tuning resolution) meaningful, you need a regulated **measure** over an
   infinite supraverse — and you must show bounce-seeded observers dominate over
   direct vacuum-fluctuation observers. This is the unsolved measure problem of
   eternal inflation, reincarnated. It threatens the observer-placement claims
   directly, not peripherally.

4. **Dimensionality (3+1).** Asserted (no 5th dimension), never derived. Why
   three large spatial dimensions is inherited.

5. **The supraverse's own origin and its time.** Q15/Q29 honestly flag this: the
   regress isn't closed. SCT needs the supraverse "old enough for equilibrium"
   but offers no account of its beginning, and the static block-universe picture
   sits uneasily with the dynamical language of "nucleation" and "equilibration."

6. **Quantum measurement.** The gravitational-decoherence-as-mini-bounce idea
   (Ch. 7) is an analogy, not a solution to the measurement problem; it would
   itself require the QG seam in (A) to be made precise.

## C. Where "assuming I'm right" is doing heavy lifting

These are *internal* to SCT — not out of scope, but places where a claimed
derivation is really an assumption or faces a known obstruction.

1. **CMB acoustic peaks from a Hawking spectrum (the existential test).** The
   peaks encode acoustic oscillations of a baryon–photon fluid at recombination.
   Hawking emission from a ~$10^{53}$ kg parent is a near-featureless thermal/
   greybody spectrum at ~$10^{-30}$ K. Redshifting a featureless spectrum does
   not obviously reproduce a structured peak series. Q16/Tier-2 names this; it is
   the most vulnerable load-bearing claim. If it can't be done, the CMB-Hawking
   identification fails regardless of the rest.

2. **Homogeneity/isotropy out of a (Kerr) black-hole interior.** Generic BH
   interiors approach the singularity *anisotropically and chaotically*
   (BKL/Mixmaster), and Kerr is rotating. Getting a universe isotropic to
   $10^{-5}$ out of that is exactly the problem inflation was invented to solve.
   SCT asserts torsion-driven expansion smooths it "like inflation," but that's
   asserted, and BKL chaos works against it.

3. **The low-entropy bounce.** Ch. 6 argues the bounce is *forced* to be
   low gravitational entropy because "high entropy needs complex structure and
   structure can't exist at $\rho_C$." But gravitational (Weyl) entropy is large
   precisely for *clumpy* configurations — and the infalling matter came from a
   structured universe full of black holes. The claim conflates thermodynamic
   smoothness with low Weyl curvature. Why the infall homogenizes to low Weyl
   *at* the bounce is not shown; this is the framework's weakest derivation, and
   it's the one the whole arrow-of-time argument rests on.

4. **Smolin tension — no actual selection of constants.** Smolin's cosmological
   natural selection *requires* constants to vary slightly and be heritable, so
   selection drives them toward black-hole fertility. SCT keeps the Standard
   Model **fixed** across all bounces (B/A above). So SCT has a *population
   distribution at fixed physics*, not Darwinian selection of constants. That
   means the hard fine-tuning (the *values* of the constants that make a
   universe black-hole-fertile in the first place) is **not** explained — only
   the mass/size distribution at given constants is. The README's "no
   fine-tuning" should be read narrowly. Either embrace fixed physics (and drop
   the Smolin-style selection framing) or introduce heritable variation (and pay
   for it with new mechanism). Right now it's having it both ways.

5. **The chirality "flip" (Mechanism A) may be coordinate relabeling.** Ch. 4
   itself concedes the geometric-inversion-implies-C argument is subtle and some
   readings give only a relabeling, not a physical matter→antimatter flip. Since
   flip is doing the work of avoiding the "hell upward" problem and preserving
   CMB thermality, a lot rides on a claim the chapter rates as shaky.

6. **Flatness, monopoles, BBN.** Inflation also fixes flatness ($\Omega\approx1$)
   and dilutes relics; BBN tightly constrains the radiation-dominated era. SCT
   must *reproduce* all of these (Q20), which is burden-matching, not
   explanation. None is shown yet.

## One-line summary

SCT is ambitious about *cosmological* puzzles (Big Bang, inflation, dark sector,
arrow of time, matter–antimatter) and largely silent on *foundational* ones
(the laws and constants, the cosmological constant, the measure, dimensionality,
QG proper). Granting all its claims, its survival hinges on three computations it
hasn't done: acoustic peaks from a bounce, isotropy out of a rotating interior,
and a genuinely low-Weyl bounce. Those are the places to push next.

\chapter{The Bounce Transform: Arrow, Charge, and Chirality}
\label{chap:chirality}

When matter crosses Cartasis, three things could in principle happen to it: its
\emph{arrow of time} could reverse, its \emph{charge} could flip (matter $\to$
antimatter, the operation $C$), and its \emph{handedness} could be filtered (one
chirality transmitted preferentially). Earlier drafts of this chapter assumed
the bounce performs a $CPT$-like flip that conveniently delivers a
matter--antimatter asymmetry. The dynamics we can actually derive say otherwise,
and the honest result is more constraining---and, in its way, more interesting.

\begin{quote}
\itshape
The minimal Einstein--Cartan bounce is $CPT$-even. It reliably turns a black hole
into a new, expanding universe (Chapter~\ref{chap:sims}), but it does \emph{not}
hand us baryogenesis. Any matter--antimatter asymmetry must be supplied, not
manufactured by the membrane.
\end{quote}

This chapter establishes that statement, then asks what would have to be added to
recover an asymmetry, and which downstream claims (the CMB as parent Hawking
radiation, dark matter as the parent's gravity through the membrane) survive.

\section{What drives the bounce is $C$, $P$, and $T$ even}

In Einstein--Cartan--Sciama--Kibble gravity, torsion is non-dynamical and can be
integrated out algebraically~\citep{hehl1976}. For Dirac matter this leaves a
four-fermion contact term in the effective Lagrangian---the Hehl--Datta
interaction,
\[
  \Delta\mathcal{L}_{\text{tors}}
    = -\,\frac{3}{16}\,\frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,
      \big(\bar\psi\gamma^5\gamma_a\psi\big)\big(\bar\psi\gamma^5\gamma^a\psi\big),
\]
the (negative) square of the axial current $j^5_a = \bar\psi\gamma^5\gamma_a\psi$.
This is precisely the term whose energy density grows as the square of the spin
density and overwhelms the (linear) gravitational attraction at $\rhoc$: it is
\emph{what makes the bounce} (Chapter~\ref{chap:cartasis}). Its discrete
symmetries are therefore the discrete symmetries of the bounce itself:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{$C$:} the axial current is charge-conjugation \emph{even} (unlike
    the vector current $\bar\psi\gamma_a\psi$, which is $C$-odd). Its square is
    $C$-even.
  \item \textbf{$P$:} under parity $j^5_0 \to -j^5_0$ and $j^5_i \to +j^5_i$, so
    the contraction $j^5_a j^{5\,a} = (j^5_0)^2 - (j^5_i)^2$ is $P$-\emph{even}.
  \item \textbf{$T$:} the same bilinear-squared structure is $T$-\emph{even}.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Result.} The interaction that drives the bounce is separately $C$-,
$P$-, and $T$-even. Three consequences follow immediately and are not
model-dependent:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{No flip.} A $C$-even interaction cannot convert matter into
    antimatter. The membrane does not charge-conjugate what passes through it.
  \item \textbf{No filter from nothing.} A $C$- and $P$-even interaction cannot
    \emph{generate} a chiral asymmetry. With zero net axial density in, it gives
    zero net axial density out.
  \item \textbf{No arrow reversal.} A $T$-even dynamics does not reverse the
    direction of proper time (see the next section).
\end{enumerate}

The bounce is real and robust; the convenient asymmetry-generating ``flip'' is
not part of it.

\section{The arrow of time: a Janus point}

In the Tier-1 and Oppenheimer--Snyder bounces (Chapter~\ref{chap:sims}) the
matter worldlines carry a single, monotonic proper time $\tau$: nothing runs
backwards, and the scale factor merely passes through a minimum. There is no
reversal of the geometric time orientation; the bounce is a smooth
\emph{continuation}, collapse $\to$ expansion.

What reverses is the \emph{thermodynamic} arrow. Infalling parent material
arrives from a structured, high-entropy universe; compression toward $\rhoc$
dismantles that structure and smooths the geometry (Weyl curvature $\to 0$), so
gravitational entropy \emph{decreases} toward the membrane. On the far side,
expansion lets structure---and eventually black holes---form again, so entropy
\emph{increases} away from it. Entropy therefore has a \emph{minimum at the
membrane}, and the locally-defined arrow points away from the bounce on both
sides. This is a \emph{Janus point}~\citep{barbour2014}: one spacetime, monotonic
proper time, two thermodynamic arrows diverging from a central entropy minimum.

The baby thus inherits its low-entropy past---the Past Hypothesis of
Chapter~\ref{chap:timearrow}---for free, with \emph{no} time flip required. The
``$r$ becomes timelike, so time inverts'' language is a statement about
Schwarzschild coordinates inside the horizon, not a physical reversal of the
matter's proper time.

\section{The fork in the road}

An entropy-minimum surface admits two geometrically distinct readings, and they
are different physics with different predictions. This is the genuine fork the
framework faces.

\subsection*{Road A --- Continuation (the derived case)}

The two sides are physically distinct, real regions---a real parent interior and
a real baby universe---joined by continuous, $CPT$-even evolution. Time
orientation is preserved, $C$ is preserved (matter stays matter), and the arrow
reflects rather than reverses. \textbf{This is what our simulated bounce is.} Its
price is honesty: the bounce supplies a universe, not an asymmetry.

\subsection*{Road B --- $CPT$ mirror (an added postulate)}

One may instead \emph{postulate} that the membrane is a $CPT$ reflection, so that
the region on the far side is the $CPT$ image of this one---reversed time
orientation, parity-flipped, charge-conjugated, hence an \emph{antimatter}
partner. This is the Boyle--Finn--Turok ``$CPT$-symmetric universe''
construction, in which the Big Bang itself is a $CPT$ mirror~\citep{boyle2018}.
It is a coherent and predictive hypothesis (it implies, for example, a definite
right-handed-neutrino dark-matter candidate), but it is an \emph{additional
discrete-symmetry assumption imposed on the bounce}, not a consequence of
Einstein--Cartan dynamics. The old ``Mechanism A: flip'' was Road~B smuggled in
as though derived.

\medskip
\noindent The framework's baseline is Road~A. Road~B remains available as an
explicit, labelled postulate to be judged on its own predictions---but it should
never again be presented as something the geometry gives for free.

\section{Where an asymmetry could actually come from}

Granting Road~A, the membrane neither creates nor flips an asymmetry. There are
exactly three honest places one could be sourced.

\paragraph{The seed (the load-bearing mechanism).} An OG universe nucleated
directly from a vacuum fluctuation inherits the specific quantum numbers of
\emph{that} fluctuation, not the vacuum average. The chirality/baryon bias of a
particular Cartasis-density fluctuation is drawn from a distribution that, by
$CPT$ invariance of the vacuum, is symmetric overall: equal numbers of
matter-biased and antimatter-biased OG lineages across the supraverse. Our
universe then has a definite bias because it descends from a definite, biased
seed. No baryogenesis mechanism is invoked; the asymmetry is \emph{inherited},
and global $CPT$ is preserved at the supraverse level. This is the only source
that survives in minimal ECSK, and it should carry the weight.

\paragraph{Mean-field amplification (not a source).} In a background that already
carries a net axial density $\langle j^5\rangle \neq 0$, the Hehl--Datta energy
depends on alignment, so a pre-existing bias is self-consistently enhanced near
$\rhoc$. This is a genuine effect, but it is an \emph{amplifier}, not a source:
$\langle j^5\rangle = 0$ in gives $0$ out. It can sharpen an inherited bias; it
cannot start one.

\paragraph{Parity-violating gravity (a derived filter, if you pay for it).} A
true chirality \emph{filter}---preferential transmission of one handedness---
requires a parity-odd ingredient. The minimal Hehl--Datta term has none, but the
gravitational action admits parity-odd extensions (the Holst term with a finite
Barbero--Immirzi parameter, or a Nieh--Yan coupling) that make torsion couple
chirally. Adding one is a specific, testable modification, and it is the honest
route to a calculable filter efficiency to compare against observation. Whether
such a term is warranted is an open question, flagged as speculative.

\section{A quantitative viability check: the chiral-vortical estimate}
\label{sec:cve-estimate}

The seed mechanism above is qualitative until one asks whether the
rotating-bounce vorticity can source an asymmetry of the \emph{observed size},
$\eta_{\mathrm{obs}}\simeq6.1\times10^{-10}$. The relevant transport is the
chiral-vortical effect: a hot relativistic chiral plasma rotating with vorticity
$\omega$ carries an axial current along its spin axis, $j^5\sim(T^2/6)\,\omega$,
parity-odd and tied to the parent's rotation. The frozen-in bias scales as
\begin{equation}
  \eta \;\sim\; C\,\frac{\omega}{T},
  \label{eq:cve-eta}
\end{equation}
with $C$ an $O(0.01\text{--}10)$ coefficient absorbing the anomaly normalisation
and the sphaleron conversion efficiency. This is a \emph{scaling estimate with
explicit assumptions}, not a derivation
(\texttt{sims/cartasis\_sims/cve\_filter.py}; \texttt{notes/cve-filter.md}).

\paragraph{The numbers.} At $\rhoc=10^{50}\,\mathrm{kg\,m^{-3}}$, the plasma
temperature from $\rhoc c^2=(g_\star\pi^2/30)T^4$ with Standard-Model
$g_\star=106.75$ is $T\simeq1.05\times10^{7}\,\mathrm{GeV}$---hot enough that
electroweak \emph{sphalerons are active}, so the $B{+}L$-violating machinery
baryogenesis needs is present. The natural bounce rate is
$\sqrt{8\pi G\rhoc/3}\simeq2.4\times10^{20}\,\mathrm{s^{-1}}$, and the controlling
small parameter at \emph{maximal} spin is
\begin{equation}
  \left.\frac{\omega}{T}\right|_{\max} \simeq 1.5\times10^{-11}.
  \label{eq:cve-omegaT}
\end{equation}
Matching \eqref{eq:cve-eta} to $\eta_{\mathrm{obs}}$ then requires
\begin{equation}
  C\cdot f_\omega \;\simeq\; 41,
  \label{eq:cve-need}
\end{equation}
where $f_\omega\le1$ is the spin fraction (\Cref{fig:cvefilter}).

\paragraph{How to read the 41---and how not to.} It is tempting to call this ``a
prediction within a factor of $41$ of the observed asymmetry.'' That overstates
it. Equation~\eqref{eq:cve-need} is not a residual error left over after a
prediction; it is the value the \emph{uncomputed} coefficient $C$ is required to
supply. There is no derived $\eta$ here. What the estimate establishes is weaker
but still real: the bare, parameter-free small number
$\omega/T\sim10^{-11}$ lands within $\sim$1.6 orders of magnitude of
$\eta_{\mathrm{obs}}$, when it could as easily have been $10^{-30}$ or $10^{+5}$.
The mechanism is in the \emph{right ballpark}. But it \emph{generically
undershoots}: since $f_\omega\le1$, Eq.~\eqref{eq:cve-need} demands $C\gtrsim41$,
which sits at or \emph{above} the top of $C$'s nominal $O(0.01\text{--}10)$ band.
Closing the gap needs near-maximal bounce vorticity \emph{and} an anomaly/sphaleron
factor pushed to its optimistic edge.

\paragraph{The keeper is directional, not numerical.} The robust output of
\eqref{eq:cve-eta} is not the magnitude but the \emph{structure}: the asymmetry is
sourced \emph{along the spin axis} and scales with bounce vorticity. This ties
baryogenesis to the same axis that appears in the CMB low-multipole alignments and
the galaxy-spin test (Chapter~\ref{chap:tests})---one axis, three phenomena. That
qualitative link survives regardless of where $C$ lands.

\paragraph{Why a bigger spin cannot rescue it (the OGU/BHU tension).} A natural
hope is that the missing factor hides in the vorticity: pick a faster-spinning
seed and $\omega/T$ climbs. It cannot, and the reason is sharp. The value in
\eqref{eq:cve-omegaT} is already the \emph{maximal-spin ceiling}: $\omega$ is
capped by the bounce rate $\sqrt{8\pi G\rhoc/3}$ (faster, and the centrifugal
barrier halts collapse before $\rhoc$), so \emph{no} seed can exceed it. The
likely vorticity is far below it. This creates a genuine tension with the
inheritance picture of Section~\ref{sec:lineage-chirality}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{If we were an OG universe} ($n=0$), our $\eta$ would be made at our
    \emph{own} bounce---but a typical OG fluctuation has \emph{small} net vorticity
    ($P_{\mathrm{birth}}$ peaked at $\omega=0$; Section~\ref{sec:spin-dist}), so
    $f_\omega\ll1$ and the estimate undershoots by far more than $41$. It is
    ``too wrong'' in the undershoot direction---and, independently, the
    dark-matter\,$+$\,dark-energy anchor (Chapter~\ref{chap:supraverse}) already
    says we are \emph{not} an OG universe ($n\ge1$).
  \item \textbf{If we are a $\mathrm{BHU}_n$} ($n\ge1$), our parent black hole can
    be near-extremal Kerr, so $f_\omega\to1$ \emph{at our own bounce}---but by the
    CPT-even result our bounce is an \emph{amplifier, not a source}: that large spin
    re-shapes an inherited bias, it does not manufacture one. The sourcing happened
    upstream, at the OG seed, whose spin is capped and typically small.
\end{itemize}
So the magnitude is largest exactly where the framework forbids sourcing (the
high-spin astrophysical bounces) and required exactly where the spin is smallest
(the OG seed). Plugging the likely OG vorticity into \eqref{eq:cve-eta} therefore
\emph{widens} the shortfall rather than closing it. The factor of $41$ must come
from somewhere other than spin:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{The coefficient $C$.} A real computation of the
    gravitational/rotational contribution to the chiral anomaly in the bounce
    metric, plus the sphaleron freeze-in before washout, could in principle deliver
    $C\sim O(10\text{--}40)$. This is the honest place the factor could live---but
    it is uncomputed, and $40$ is above the nominal estimate.
  \item \textbf{The density $\rhoc$.} Since $\omega/T\propto\rhoc^{1/4}$, gaining
    $41$ needs $\rhoc$ higher by $41^4\approx3\times10^{6}$, i.e.\
    $\rhoc\sim3\times10^{56}\,\mathrm{kg\,m^{-3}}$. This runs the \emph{wrong way}:
    the composition argument of \texttt{notes/rho-c-limits.md} ($\rhoc\propto
    m_f^2$, lighter spin carriers past deconfinement) pushes $\rhoc$ \emph{down},
    deepening the shortfall. A real tension to track, not a free lever.
  \item \textbf{A parity-violating gravitational term.} The Holst (finite
    Barbero--Immirzi) or Nieh--Yan coupling supplies the genuinely $P$-odd
    ingredient a true filter needs, changing the mechanism rather than rescaling it.
    This is the speculative route flagged above and in
    \texttt{notes/rho-c-limits.md}.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Verdict.} Viable, spin-tied, and probably needs help. The
chiral-vortical estimate earns the directional claim outright and the
order-of-magnitude claim honestly; it does \emph{not} yet predict
$\eta\approx6\times10^{-10}$, and the missing factor of $\sim41$ is a debt owed to
a calculation of $C$, not a small discrepancy already paid.

\section{The surviving remnant: a needle at one part per billion}
\label{sec:remnant}

It is worth being careful about \emph{what the membrane does to matter}, because
the word ``filter'' has been doing covert work. A filter sorts a pre-existing
population, transmitting some and rejecting the rest. That is not the picture the
dynamics give. At $\rhoc$ the infalling material is thermalised at
$\sim10^{20}\,\mathrm{K}$ into a near-symmetric relativistic bath in which every
species is pair-produced (Chapter~\ref{chap:darksector}); the parent's particular
matter content is erased. What emerges on the far side is a freshly \emph{extruded}
universe---the membrane compresses parent material to $\rhoc$ and extrudes an
expanding interior---carrying through only what the dynamics protect. The
operative metaphor is extrusion through a die, not filtration through a sieve.

\paragraph{What is protected.} Sphaleron processes at the bounce temperature erase
$B{+}L$ but \emph{cannot touch} $B{-}L$. So whatever net $B{-}L$ the infalling
material carries passes through the extrusion as a conserved remnant---this is the
``extra matter,'' and its survival is guaranteed by a conservation law, not by the
efficiency of any mechanism. (How much \emph{additional} skew leaks through is the
open part: the crossing lasts only $\sim4\times10^{-21}\,\mathrm{s}$, so the
$B{+}L$ washout is plausibly \emph{incomplete}, and incomplete washout lets more of
the inherited asymmetry survive than an equilibrium estimate would allow.)

\paragraph{The needle.} The remnant must thread a fine target. The observed
$\eta\simeq6\times10^{-10}$ is $\sim$0.6 parts per billion of net baryon per
photon. Extrude \emph{too much} surviving asymmetry and the baby is over-baryonic,
not ours; extrude \emph{too little} and it annihilates to a sterile furnace. The
viable window sits at $\sim$1\,ppb.

\paragraph{Why inheritance threads it for free---if we are not the OGU.} Here the
generation structure pays off. The dark-matter\,$+$\,dark-energy anchor
(Chapter~\ref{chap:supraverse}) says we are a $\mathrm{BHU}_n$ with $n\ge1$, not an
original-generation universe. Our parent is therefore \emph{already} a universe
that completed baryogenesis and annihilated down to its own baryon-to-entropy
ratio $\sim$1\,ppb. A near-entropy-conserving extrusion simply passes that ratio
through: the baby inherits $\eta_{\mathrm{baby}}\approx\eta_{\mathrm{parent}}\sim
10^{-9}$ \emph{without the regenerator having to manufacture it}. On this reading
the factor-of-$41$ shortfall of Section~\ref{sec:cve-estimate} is \emph{not our
problem}---it is the OGU's, the one universe in each lineage that had no parent to
inherit from and had to extrude the asymmetry from a symmetric bath. We merely
carry down what was made once, at the top.

\paragraph{The competition that decides the magnitude---and the lineage.} Two
effects fight at every crossing:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Inherited remnant}, $\sim\eta_{\mathrm{parent}}$, \emph{diluted} if
    the bounce floods in new entropy (black-hole formation is hugely
    entropy-generating, and if the CMB is parent Hawking inflow,
    Section~\ref{sec:cmb-hawking}, that inflow is new photons in the denominator).
    Strong dilution drives $\eta$ \emph{down} the lineage---a ``hell downward.''
  \item \textbf{Fresh extrusion skew}, $\sim C\,(\omega/T)\lesssim C\times
    1.5\times10^{-11}$, set by the \emph{local} bounce vorticity and sign.
\end{enumerate}
The observed asymmetry is roughly $\eta_{\mathrm{baby}}\approx\max(\text{diluted
inheritance},\ \text{fresh skew})$. Crucially, the \emph{same} competition fixes
whether lineages are chirally pure: if the inherited remnant dominates, the sign is
protected and descendants share the founder's handedness
(Chapter~\ref{chap:supraverse}, Section~\ref{sec:lineage-chirality}); if a
high-spin bounce's fresh skew dominates \emph{and opposes} the inherited sign, the
sign re-rolls and the lineage \emph{mixes}. So ``how much remnant survives'' is not
a side question---it is the hinge on which both the asymmetry magnitude and the
purity of lineages turn. Quantifying it requires the bounce entropy budget, which
is unaddressed; we flag it as an open question
(Appendix~\ref{app:openq}, Q16a) and as the natural next calculation.

\section{What survives, and what needs massage}

It is worth being explicit about which downstream claims depend on the discarded
flip and which do not.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Black hole $\to$ universe: intact.} The central result---a torsion
    bounce reliably producing an inverse-bubble universe behind the horizon---is
    independent of all of this (Chapter~\ref{chap:sims}). The membrane is a
    reliable cosmogonic engine.
  \item \textbf{CMB $=$ parent Hawking radiation: intact, recast.} Under Road~A the
    radiation crossing in is same-charge thermal radiation; there is no
    chirality-distortion signature to predict, and the old argument that ``flip
    preserves thermality, so observations favour flip'' is withdrawn. The
    identification stands or falls on the spectral and geometric calculation of
    Chapter~\ref{chap:darksector}, not on the transform.
  \item \textbf{Dark matter as the parent's gravity through the membrane: intact in
    principle.} The gravitational influence of parent-side mass-energy on the
    baby does not require a flip. What \emph{does} require massaging is the
    specific ``dark matter $=$ filter-rejected \emph{antimatter}, with
    $f\approx1/6$'' story: in minimal ECSK there is no chirality filter to do the
    rejecting, so either (i) the rejected component is ordinary parent matter held
    at the membrane by the junction conditions (no antimatter needed), or (ii)
    one invokes the parity-violating extension above. State which; do not assume
    the filter.
  \item \textbf{Matter--antimatter asymmetry: inherited, and that is enough.} It is
    \emph{selected once at the OG seed} and passed down each lineage, possibly
    amplified, rather than generated at the bounce. This replaces the old
    ``alternating-chirality lineage'' picture, and it resolves the ``hell upward''
    problem more cleanly than that picture did: because the bias is inherited from
    a single founding seed rather than re-won at each crossing, there are no
    near-symmetric intermediate ancestors to climb past (Section~\ref{sec:lineage-chirality}).
    Lineage viability reduces to one condition on the seed---enough net vorticity
    to clear baryogenesis---which is a derivable seed statistic
    (Section~\ref{sec:spin-dist}), not a bounce-induced sign alternation.
\end{itemize}

\section{Status and open questions}

The reassessment trades a convenient story for a defensible one. What is settled:
the bounce is $CPT$-even; the arrow is a Janus reflection, not a reversal; the
asymmetry is not a free output of the membrane. What is open, and where the
framework needs development:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Quantify the mean-field amplification gain as a function of density and
    inherited bias (amplifier-not-source, made numerical).
  \item Decide whether to adopt a parity-violating gravitational term, and if so
    compute the resulting filter efficiency from first principles.
  \item Quantify how ongoing biased accretion over the parent's life integrates
    the per-bounce filter (Chapter~\ref{chap:supraverse}); test whether it closes
    the single-bounce shortfall.
  \item \emph{(Resolved.)} The ``hell upward'' problem is dissolved by
    \emph{inheritance from a single OG seed}: the asymmetry is selected once, at
    the original-generation nucleation, and passed down each lineage unweakened
    (amplifier-not-source), so climbing toward ancestors does not dilute it and
    there are no near-symmetric sterile intermediate ancestors---only the OG seed
    and, beyond it, the void (Chapter~\ref{chap:supraverse},
    Section~\ref{sec:lineage-chirality}). Lineages are chirally pure; viability is
    decided once, at the seed's spin (Section~\ref{sec:spin-dist}). Road~A
    (continuation) is adopted as the baseline and propagated through
    Chapters~\ref{chap:baby} and~\ref{chap:supraverse}; Road~B remains an explicit
    optional postulate.
\end{itemize}

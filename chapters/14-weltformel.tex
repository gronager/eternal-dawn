\chapter{The Weltformel}
\label{chap:weltformel}

The previous chapters established structure: the torsiton exists, mass is configurational,
colour is forced, generations are a capped geometric ladder, the electroweak boson masses
follow from one condensate scale. This chapter asks the strict question behind all of it---the
question Heisenberg's nonlinear-spinor programme asked and could not answer in the 1960s. Can
the whole spectrum be computed from the constants of Nature alone, with no inserted Yukawa, no
electroweak vacuum value, no fitted well? The honest answer is: the \emph{structure}, yes; the
absolute numbers, not on a laptop---and the gap between them is itself instructive.

\section{Far fewer parameters than the Standard Model}
\label{sec:params}

First the bookkeeping. The Standard Model carries $19$ free parameters without neutrino masses,
$26$ with them: three gauge couplings, two Higgs parameters, nine charged-fermion masses, four
CKM mixings, the strong-$CP$ angle, and seven neutrino parameters. The great majority---the
nine masses, four mixings, seven neutrino numbers---are pure insertion: nothing in the Standard
Model explains why the electron weighs what it does; the number is measured and typed in.

In the torsiton picture each of these blocks becomes, in principle, a consequence. The gauge
couplings trace to the connection (colour forced by the label, the weak sector the one
four-fermion coupling); the Higgs sector is composite, its scale $v$ generated by dimensional
transmutation rather than inserted; the masses and mixings are wavefunction overlaps. In the
ideal case the particle sector adds \emph{zero} new fundamental parameters beyond the
$G,\hbar,c$ the rest of the book already uses (Figure~\ref{fig:params}). The honest residuals
are stated in the same breath: the gauge group ($SU(3)$ taken as the Pauli minimum), the
strong-$CP$ angle (not addressed), and---the load-bearing one---the \emph{numbers}, which are
owed to the lattice. The reduction is real in structure; the proof is not yet in hand.

\section{The matrix from $G$, $\hbar$, $c$}
\label{sec:matrix}

Now the computation itself, with the constants of Nature as the only dimensionful inputs.
Because the four-fermion coupling \emph{is} gravity ($\kappa = 8\pi G/c^4$), the theory sets its
own scale: the natural mass of a soliton bound by it is the Planck mass,
\[
  M_{\mathrm{Pl}}c^2 = \sqrt{\frac{\hbar c^5}{G}} \approx 1.22\times10^{19}~\mathrm{GeV}.
\]
The dimensionless structure is then turned out by the crank: the self-consistent soliton
(no fitted shape) gives the eigenlevels and wavefunctions; their substrate overlaps give the
generation factor; the charge and colour handles---built from the $SU(3)$ Casimir, the electric
charge $Q$, and the weak isospin $T$, with no fit---give the tower factor. Assembling
\[
  m(T,n) = M_{\mathrm{Pl}}\;\hat h(T)\;\hat O(n)
\]
produces a full $3\times4$ fermion matrix, and the same soliton's composite scales give $W$,
$Z$, and $H$ (Figure~\ref{fig:abinitio}). It is a real, ab-initio spectrum.

The charges do exactly what they should. A fermion's coupling ``handle'' on the condensate is
$\hat h \sim \alpha_s\,C_2^{\mathrm{colour}} + \alpha_{\mathrm{em}}\,Q^2 + \alpha_w\,T(T{+}1)$,
built from group theory and the measured gauge couplings---no fitted per-tower knob. Quarks sit
above leptons because $\sim\!85\%$ of a quark's handle is colour, the channel leptons lack. And
a right-handed neutrino is a total singlet: colour $0$, charge $0$, weak $0$, hence handle
exactly zero, hence massless at leading order. The neutrino is light \emph{because of its
charges}, not by a tuned small number---the one piece of the mass problem the framework settles
cleanly and without fit.

What the laptop does \emph{not} produce is the absolute number: the $0.511~\mathrm{MeV}$, or the
inter-tower factors. Those are the non-perturbative normalisations of a strongly-coupled
sector---the lattice. Two gaps remain, and they are different in kind.

\section{The structure gap}

The first gap is the generation spread. The simple self-consistent well, overlapped with its own
broad condensate, gives a spread of only $\sim\!3$ where the towers span $10^3$--$10^4$. This
gap closes with the substrate overlap of Section~\ref{sec:generations}: overlapping each level
against the sharp substrate (its width the well's healing length, derived) steepens the spread
to $\sim\!10^3$ and beyond, the observed size, at natural well depths. The structure gap is
therefore tractable---a refinement of the overlap, not a missing mechanism.

\section{The scale gap}
\label{sec:scale-gap}

The second gap is the factor $\sim\!10^{17}$ between the Planck-scaled matrix and the observed
masses. This is not a fine-tuning to despair over; it is $e^{-40}$, the signature of
\emph{dimensional transmutation}. The same mechanism already makes $\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}$ sit
$10^{19}$ below the Planck scale from an $O(1/30)$ coupling, and it resolves the scale gap here
too---but it requires a second coupling.

In pure Einstein--Cartan, torsion is algebraic: glued to $G$, with no dynamics of its own, so
the only scale available is $M_{\mathrm{Pl}}$, which is why the naive torsiton lands there. The
natural extension---Poincar\'e gauge gravity---lets the spin connection \emph{propagate},
carrying its own gauge coupling $g_T$, independent of Newton's $G$. This is the second coupling.
If $g_T$ is asymptotically free, it is weak in the ultraviolet and condenses in the infrared at
\[
  \Lambda = M_{\mathrm{Pl}}\,\exp\!\left(-\frac{2\pi}{b_0\, g_T^2}\right),
\]
exponentially below the Planck scale. The torsiton binds at $\Lambda$, not $M_{\mathrm{Pl}}$.
An entirely ordinary coupling, $g_T^2 \sim 0.02$ (weaker than electromagnetism), reproduces the
observed electroweak-to-Planck ratio (Figure~\ref{fig:scalegap}); rescaling the matrix from
$M_{\mathrm{Pl}}$ to $\Lambda$ pins the top at $173~\mathrm{GeV}$ and drops the whole heavy end
into the observed $10$--$170~\mathrm{GeV}$ band. The scale gap closes.

This costs an axiom---propagating torsion is more than minimal Einstein--Cartan, and the book's
parsimony rule demands the flag. But it is a principled addition, not a free knob: it is the one
thing that gives the spin-gravity field its own quantum scale, it converts the hierarchy from a
mystery into the generic output of an asymptotically-free coupling, and the single scale
$\Lambda$ it generates is the $v$ of the electroweak chapter, the $f_\pi$ of the parameter
count, and the $\Lambda$ of genesis---one transmuted scale, used everywhere. Instead of
inserting $v=246~\mathrm{GeV}$, one derives it.

\section{Where the programme stands}

Turn the crank with the constants of Nature, and the whole spectrum falls out with the right
\emph{structure}---three generations, four towers, the charge ordering, the forced-light
neutrino---and two well-posed gaps: the structure gap (the substrate overlap, tractable) and
the scale gap (dimensional transmutation of $g_T$, resolved in principle). This is exactly
Heisenberg's outcome, sharpened: not twenty inserted constants, but two computations---one
lattice, one a running coupling. Whether the absolute numbers emerge when those computations are
done is the open verdict of the whole Part; the Appendix states the lattice targets precisely
enough to be picked up. The framework predicts that the spectrum \emph{is} computable from the
torsion-gravity dynamics; it is honest that the decimals await the supercomputer.

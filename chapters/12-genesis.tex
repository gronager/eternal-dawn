\chapter{Ab Initio Genesis}
\label{chap:genesis}

This is the thermal history of matter told in the language of \emph{Eternal Dawn}: not what
happened in the first $10^{-21}$ second of a finished Big Bang, but what happens, still and
continuously, as the extruded field crosses the bounce membrane and cools. ``Ab initio''
twice over---from first principles (no inserted particle content; everything follows from the
one field's phase diagram) and from the beginning (the literal post-membrane origin). And, in
keeping with the book's central thesis: genesis is \emph{ongoing}. The membrane is a
persistent surface at our past horizon, the parent is still extruding field across it, and the
sequence below is not a clock that ran once but a standing gradient in radius and temperature
that the inflowing field climbs down for as long as the parent feeds.

\section{The one substance, cooling}

Everything in Parts~I and~II rests on a single object: the gravity--torsion field, the same
field whose repulsive core makes the bounce. Matter is not a separate ingredient sprinkled on
spacetime; it is that field, knotted into torsitons. So genesis is not the \emph{creation} of
particles from nothing---it is the \emph{condensation} of particles out of a field that was
already there, as it is squeezed across the membrane and allowed to cool. There is one
substance; genesis is its phase diagram. What we call ``the early universe'' is, in this
picture, the hot end of the standing gradient: the layer nearest the membrane where the field
has not yet cooled enough to condense.

\section{The order of operations}

Read the following as a descent in temperature $T$---equivalently, outward from the membrane,
since the field cools with distance from the hot extrusion surface. Each stage is a threshold
the cooling field crosses.

\paragraph{Stage 0 --- at the membrane: the symmetric, massless field.}
Right at the membrane the field is at the bounce density $\rhoc$ and hottest; the chiral
condensate is melted, $v\to0$. By the configurational-mass result
(Section~\ref{sec:configurational-mass}), when the condensate vanishes so does mass: the
field crossing the membrane carries no rest mass---a hot, symmetric, effectively massless
gravity--torsion plasma. The quantities conserved across the membrane are exactly those
Part~I keeps: $P^\mu$, $B\!-\!L$, $Q$, spin---not mass, which has not yet switched on.

\paragraph{Stage 1 --- the condensate forms: mass switches on.}
As the field cools past the chiral scale, the symmetric phase becomes unstable and the vacuum
condenses, $v:0\to f_\pi$, in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio manner \citep{nambu1961}. This is the
single most important event in the particle genesis and the moment mass appears in the universe:
every torsiton's rest mass is the condensate-bound field energy
(Eq.~\ref{eq:configmass}), so before this nothing weighs anything and after it everything does.
The scale $f_\pi\simeq\Lambda$ is the infrared condensation scale set once in
Chapter~\ref{chap:solitons}, not an inserted number. The dawn lights up---the field acquires
weight---precisely here.

\paragraph{Stage 2 --- the torsitons crystallise.}
With a condensate to bind against, the field's localized self-bound solutions become stable:
the torsitons of Chapter~\ref{chap:solitons}. The structureless plasma crystallises into
matter---standing waves freezing out of a cooling continuum, as droplets condense from a
cooling vapour. Each torsiton is a quantum of matter; the generations are its internal rungs,
a geometric ladder with a finite cap, so genesis produces three families and stops.

\paragraph{Stage 3 --- colour and confinement turn on.}
The torsitons carry the three-valued Pauli label the algebra forces
(Chapter~\ref{chap:forces}). As the field cools further, the strong channel passes from the
screening, perturbative regime into the confining one: the condensate, acting as a dual
superconductor, expels colour-electric flux into Nielsen--Olesen flux tubes
\citep{nielsen1973}. Above this the coloured torsitons roam in a deconfined plasma; below it
they are roped together and only colour singlets survive.

\paragraph{Stage 4 --- hadrons.}
Once the tubes are on, an isolated coloured torsiton would cost an infinite tube, so the field
arranges its quanta into the singlets the colour algebra allows: mesons (torsiton $+$
anti-torsiton) and baryons (three torsitons, one of each colour). The protons and neutrons of
the universe condense here.

\paragraph{Stage 5 --- the light elements.}
Cooler still, the baryons assemble into the first nuclei (standard big-bang nucleosynthesis
\citep{cooke2018,pitrou2018}), and the field's genesis hands off to ordinary nuclear and atomic
physics. In \emph{Eternal
Dawn} this is the outer, cool layer of the standing gradient---and, unlike in $\Lambda$CDM, it
is continuously re-supplied from within by the ongoing extrusion, not a frozen relic of a
one-time event.

\section{The genesis, simulated}
\label{sec:genesis-sim}

The cascade above is not only a story to be told; it runs as a simulation. Cooling a chiral fluid
through the single transition of Stage~1, on a lattice, one watches the stages play out in order:
the condensate switching on, the field crystallising into torsitons (Stage~2), the coloured ones
roped into colour-singlet baryons (Stages~3--4), the colourless ones left free as the leptons. The
defining feature of \emph{Eternal Dawn}---that this is \emph{one} transition, not the Standard
Model's staged electroweak-then-QCD sequence---is exactly what the simulation shows: every species
acquires its mass at the same threshold, as the one condensate $v(T)$ grows
(Figure~\ref{fig:genesis-horizon}). The colourless leptons and sub-eV neutrinos and the confined
baryon octet and decuplet fan out together, eleven orders of magnitude in mass, from a single cooling
of a single field.

The crystallisation itself (Stage~2) is a three-dimensional quench: starting from the hot, symmetric
field and lowering the temperature, the localized self-bound solutions freeze out of the continuum as
droplets condense from a cooling vapour---standing torsiton knots appearing where the field was
structureless (Figure~\ref{fig:genesis-quench3d}). Their number follows the Kibble--Zurek scaling of
a quench \citep{kibble1976,zurek1985}: a faster cooling traps more knots, the genesis abundance of
matter set by the rate at which the parent feeds the membrane.

\paragraph{Honest scope.} These are \emph{classical stochastic} effective-field simulations: they
carry the mechanism and the order of magnitude---which species, in what order, with relative masses
good to $\sim\!30\%$ and the formation abundance---not the exact quantum spectrum, which is the
lattice's word (Chapter~\ref{chap:weltformel}, Appendix~\ref{app:lattice}). The single owed input is
the same one the whole Part turns on: the scale $\Lambda$ and the bag sharpness $s_T$
(Chapter~\ref{chap:solitons}). The simulation runs now on the calibrated values and re-runs, the same
code, for the absolute-MeV prediction the day the lattice returns $\Lambda$---the structure is the
content; the decimals are owed.

\section{The same diagram as the bounce}

The descent---symmetric/massless $\to$ condensate $\to$ mass $\to$ torsitons $\to$
confinement---is the particle-scale image of the cosmological bounce run as a phase diagram
instead of a trajectory. At the membrane the field is in its melted, symmetric, massless phase
($\rhoc$, $v=0$, the bounce core); as it cools it condenses and acquires weight; the matter we
are made of is the crystallised, cooled phase. The bounce melts matter back to the symmetric
field on the way in, and genesis re-condenses it on the way out: catharsis and genesis are the
two halves of one crossing.

These two halves close onto a single map. The mass--radius plane (\Cref{fig:massradius})
places every object the framework names---the void seed, the black holes, our own universe,
the firstborn OGU, and the twelve fermions this chapter crystallises---between the same two
exclusions, the torsion--gravity membrane and the Compton limit, with the TARDIS (a black
hole read as a universe from the inside) as its organizing idea. The fermions genesis
produces sit on the Compton limit at lower right, on the very same plane as the cosmology
they are born inside: the particle cascade of this chapter and the bounce cosmology of
Part~I are, literally, one diagram.

One honest caveat survives, the density question of Chapter~\ref{chap:weltformel} (and the
Appendix): whether the density at which a particle-scale torsiton stabilises equals the
cosmological $\rhoc$, or is related to it by a computable factor, is the quantitative seam
between this chapter and Part~I---stated plainly rather than papered over.

## The one substance, cooling

Everything in Parts II–III rests on a single object: the gravity–torsion field of
Einstein–Cartan, the same field whose repulsive core makes the bounce in Part I. Matter is
not a separate ingredient sprinkled onto spacetime; it is that field, knotted into
self-bound standing waves (the solitons of the previous chapters). So "genesis" is not the
*creation* of particles out of nothing — it is the **condensation** of particles out of a
field that was already there, as that field is squeezed across the membrane and then
allowed to cool. There is one substance. Genesis is its phase diagram.

Hold the central thesis in view while reading this. In ΛCDM, genesis is an event: the
Big Bang finishes, nucleosynthesis closes, and the particle content freezes, all in the
deep past. In Eternal Dawn the membrane is a **persistent surface** at our past horizon and
the parent is *still extruding* field across it. So the sequence below is not a clock that
ran once; it is a **standing gradient in radius/temperature** that the inflowing field
climbs down, continuously, for as long as the parent feeds. Our dawn is ongoing — and
genesis, the part of the dawn where the field crystallises into matter, is ongoing with it.
What we call "the early universe" is, in this picture, just the **hot end of that gradient**,
the layer nearest the membrane where the field has not yet cooled enough to condense.

## The order of operations (down the temperature gradient)

Read this as a descent in temperature $T$ — equivalently, outward from the membrane, since
the field cools as it gets further from the hot extrusion surface. Each stage is a
threshold the cooling field crosses, and each one is a result the laptop programme of the
previous chapters already touched.

**Stage 0 — At the membrane: the symmetric, massless field.**
Right at the bounce membrane the field is at the bounce density $\rho_C$ and hottest. Here
the chiral condensate is *melted*: $v \to 0$. By the configurational-mass result
(`chiral_soliton.py`: mass $\propto v$), **when the condensate vanishes, mass vanishes**.
So the field crossing the membrane carries no rest mass — it is a hot, symmetric, effectively
massless gravity–torsion plasma. This is the ED reading of "the early universe was a hot
symmetric phase": not a soup of pre-existing massless particles, but the *one field* above
its condensation threshold. The quantities that are conserved across the membrane are
exactly the ones Part I keeps — energy–momentum $P^\mu$, $B\!-\!L$, charge $Q$, spin —
**not** mass, which is configurational and has not switched on yet.

**Stage 1 — The condensate forms: chiral symmetry breaks, mass switches on.**
As the field cools past the chiral scale, the vacuum can no longer hold the symmetric
phase: it condenses, $v: 0 \to f_\pi$. This is the single most important event in the
particle genesis, and it is the moment **mass appears in the universe**. By the
configurational result, the rest mass of every soliton is $\sim$94% bound field energy
proportional to this condensate; before Stage 1 nothing weighs anything, after it everything
does. The scale $f_\pi$ is *not put in by hand* — it is generated by dimensional
transmutation of the torsion coupling (the parameter-count chapter), the way $\Lambda_{QCD}$
is generated in the Standard Model. So genesis sets its own mass scale. In ED language:
**the dawn lights up — the field acquires weight — precisely here.**

**Stage 2 — The solitons crystallise: matter as standing waves.**
With a condensate to bind against, the field's localized, self-bound solutions become
stable: the solitons of `self_consistent.py` (the Hartree loop converges) and
`soliton.py`. The cooling field, which above Stage 1 was a structureless plasma, now
**crystallises into particles** — knots of field that hold themselves together because the
condensate outside costs more energy than the restored core inside (the bag). This is the
literal "condensation of matter": standing waves freezing out of a cooling continuum, the
way droplets condense out of a cooling vapour. Each soliton is a quantum of matter; the
generations are its internal excitation rungs (`generations.py`), a geometric ladder with a
finite cap (`generations_cap.py`) — so genesis produces *three* families and stops, not
because three was inserted but because the S-budget runs out.

**Stage 3 — Colour and confinement turn on: the field organises into tubes.**
The solitons carry the three-valued Pauli label that the algebra forces (`fierz.py`,
`color_force.py`: the colour factors are rigorous and equal QCD's). As the field cools
further, the strong channel passes from the screening, perturbative regime (overlaps
$\to 0$) into the **confining** regime: the condensate, acting as a dual superconductor,
expels colour-electric flux into Nielsen–Olesen tubes (`flux_tube.py`, $\sigma \sim 2\pi
v^2$). This is the ED reading of the QCD phase transition / confinement–deconfinement
crossover: **above it the coloured solitons roam free in a deconfined plasma; below it they
are roped together by flux tubes** and only colour-singlet combinations survive. The same
condensate that switched mass on at Stage 1 supplies the string tension here — one
substrate, both jobs.

**Stage 4 — Hadrons: only the colour-singlets survive.**
Once the tubes are on, an isolated coloured soliton would cost an infinite tube to exist,
so the field arranges its quanta into the singlets the colour algebra allows: mesons
(soliton + anti-soliton) and baryons (three solitons, one of each colour) — the no-free-quark,
no-free-diquark result of the colour-factor chapter. **The protons and neutrons of the
universe condense here**, as the colour-neutral bound states the flux tubes permit. By this
stage the field that crossed the membrane massless and symmetric has become recognisable
matter: massive, coloured-then-confined, family-structured.

**Stage 5 — The light elements: BBN on the cooled gradient.**
Cooler still, the baryons assemble into the first nuclei (standard big-bang
nucleosynthesis), and the field's genesis hands off to ordinary nuclear and atomic physics.
In ED this is the *outer, cool layer* of the standing gradient — the part of the
post-membrane field that has had the most time/distance to cool. ΛCDM and ED agree on the
physics here; they disagree only on the *framing* — ED says this layer is continuously
re-supplied from within by the ongoing extrusion, not a frozen relic of a one-time event.

## Why this is the same diagram as the cosmological bounce

The descent above — symmetric/massless $\to$ condensate $\to$ mass $\to$ solitons $\to$
confinement — is the **particle-scale image of the cosmological bounce** of Part I, run as
a phase diagram instead of a trajectory. At the membrane the field is in its melted,
symmetric, massless phase (the bounce core, $\rho_C$, $v=0$); as it cools it condenses and
acquires weight (mass switches on); the matter we are made of is the crystallised,
cooled phase. The mass–and–the–bounce bridge chapter argued that *mass is what the bounce
does not conserve*; genesis is the constructive side of the same statement — **the bounce
melts matter back to the symmetric field on the way in, and genesis re-condenses it on the
way out.** Catharsis (Chapter 2) and genesis are the two halves of one crossing.

The one honest caveat is the density mismatch flagged in the lattice punch-list (L5): the
bare Einstein–Cartan coupling naively puts a *particle*-scale soliton near the Planck mass
unless the relevant binding density differs from the *cosmological* $\rho_C$. Whether the
particle-genesis condensation density and the cosmological bounce density are the same
number, or related by a computable factor, is owed to the lattice — it is the quantitative
seam between this chapter and Part I, stated plainly rather than papered over.

## What genesis buys, in one sentence

Every threshold above is a *consequence of the one field cooling*, not a separately tuned
epoch: the mass scale, the family count, the colour structure, the confinement scale all
fall out of the gravity–torsion field's own phase diagram with no new parameters (the
parameter-count chapter). That is the payoff of telling genesis in ED's language — **the
thermal history of matter and the thermal history of the cosmos are the same descent down
the same field's temperature gradient, and the dawn that lights it is still going on.**

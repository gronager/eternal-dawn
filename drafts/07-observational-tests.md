# 07 — Observational Tests

## Overview

SCT makes specific predictions that can in principle distinguish it from ΛCDM and other standard frameworks. This file catalogs them in rough order of how testable they are with current and near-future observational capabilities.

## Tier 1: Already-observed potential signatures

These are existing observations that may already be evidence for SCT but are conventionally interpreted otherwise.

### 1.1 Galaxy rotation asymmetry (Shamir 2025)

JWST JADES survey shows 2:1 asymmetry in galaxy rotation directions, peaking near Galactic poles. Statistical significance 3.39σ.

**SCT prediction:** Universe inherits preferred axis from parent’s rotation. Galaxy rotation should show a hemisphere-based asymmetry aligned with parent’s spin axis.

**Status:** Hint, not confirmation. Larger samples from ongoing JWST surveys will sharpen this in the next few years. If the asymmetry persists at higher significance and the Doppler-bias alternative is ruled out, this is direct evidence for parent-imprinted axis.

### 1.2 “Axis of evil” in CMB

Low-multipole CMB anomalies show suspicious alignments between the quadrupole, octupole, and the ecliptic. Statistical significance ~2-3σ.

**SCT prediction:** Parent rotation imprints anisotropy on the bounce membrane, which manifests as low-multipole CMB anisotropies aligned with parent’s rotation axis.

**Status:** Long-standing anomaly. Conventional explanations (foreground contamination, statistical fluke) haven’t fully resolved it. Could be SCT signature.

### 1.3 Evolving dark energy (DESI 2024)

DESI year-1 BAO results favor $w_0 w_a$ models with $w_0 > -1$ and $w_a < 0$ over strict $\Lambda$ at ~$2-3\sigma$. Suggests dark energy density evolves.

**SCT prediction:** Dark energy reflects parent accretion history, which is generically non-constant. Specifically, observed evolution toward weaker dark energy at recent times might indicate parent has crossed into Hawking-dominated regime.

**Status:** Statistical tension, not confirmation. Years 2-5 of DESI data will sharpen this. If evolving dark energy is confirmed at $> 5\sigma$, ΛCDM is in trouble and SCT-like models become favored.

### 1.4 Hubble tension

Persistent ~5σ disagreement between early-universe ($H_0 \approx 67$) and local ($H_0 \approx 73$) measurements.

**SCT prediction:** Bounce dynamics modify the relationship between early and late expansion. Specific calculation needed, but the framework has degrees of freedom to potentially resolve the tension.

**Status:** Calculational work needed. SCT must reproduce both CMB physics and local measurements consistently. If it can, this is a major validation.

### 1.5 $\sigma_8$ tension

Lower-than-expected matter clustering inferred from low-redshift measurements vs. CMB-extrapolated predictions.

**SCT prediction:** Dark matter is geometric (parent’s influence through membrane), not particulate. Its effective clustering behavior may differ from CDM expectations.

**Status:** Active observational research. SCT-based dark matter modeling needed.

### 1.6 JWST high-redshift massive galaxies

JWST observations of unexpectedly massive galaxies at $z > 10$, challenging standard structure formation timelines.

**SCT prediction:** Bounce dynamics might allow earlier structure formation than standard inflation, since the post-bounce expansion phase is different.

**Status:** Active research. Calculations of structure formation in bounce cosmology needed.

## Tier 2: Near-future testable

These can be tested with planned observational programs in the next 5-15 years.

### 2.1 CMB spectral details

If CMB is parent Hawking radiation, the spectrum should be perfect Planck (which it is, to $10^{-5}$). But subtle deviations or correlations might exist:

- Subtle spectral features from membrane geometry
- Correlations between CMB temperature and dark matter distribution
- Specific polarization patterns from rotating parent

**Tests:** Next-generation CMB experiments (CMB-S4, LiteBIRD) will improve constraints on CMB spectrum and polarization by orders of magnitude.

### 2.2 Cosmic neutrino background structure

The cosmic neutrino background (CνB) is predicted by standard cosmology but not yet directly observed. If observed (PTOLEMY-class experiments):

- Pure-flip framework predicts left-handed neutrinos in our convention
- Strong-filter framework predicts fewer-than-expected antineutrino-like component
- Standard cosmology predicts approximately equal $\nu$ and $\bar\nu$

**Tests:** Direct CνB detection is decades away with current technology, but is being actively pursued.

### 2.3 Primordial gravitational waves

Inflation predicts a specific tensor-to-scalar ratio $r$. Bounce cosmology predicts different (typically smaller) $r$.

**Tests:** LiteBIRD will constrain $r$ to $\sim 10^{-3}$ level. If observed at higher values, inflation favored. If observed lower or absent, bounce cosmology favored.

### 2.4 Dark matter direct detection (negative result)

SCT predicts no fundamental dark matter particles. WIMP, axion, sterile neutrino, and other direct detection experiments should continue to find nothing.

**Tests:** Ongoing programs (LZ, XENONnT, future generations) will probe progressively lower cross-sections. Continued null results are weak evidence for geometric (non-particulate) dark matter.

### 2.5 Specific BAO and large-scale structure tests

The relationship between baryon acoustic oscillations and dark matter clustering should follow specific SCT-predicted patterns if dark matter is geometric. Discrepancies with ΛCDM at large scales might be observable.

**Tests:** DESI, Euclid, Roman Space Telescope will map large-scale structure with unprecedented precision.

## Tier 3: Future testable, requires major advances

### 3.1 Gravitational decoherence experiments

Tests of Diósi-Penrose gravitational collapse (or our preferred reinterpretation: gravitational decoherence) at mesoscopic scales.

**Tests:** Bouwmeester, Aspelmeyer-class proposals using mesoscopic mirror superpositions or matter-wave interferometers. Within reach in the next 1-2 decades.

If gravitational decoherence is confirmed at predicted rates, this strengthens the bridge between bounce cosmology and quantum mechanics that we’ve argued for.

### 3.2 Information-theoretic tests of dark matter

If dark matter is entangled with the parent through the bounce, sufficiently sensitive measurements might detect specific entanglement structures (correlations between dark matter and Hawking-radiation-like backgrounds, etc.).

**Tests:** Highly speculative. Requires precision quantum-cosmological measurements not currently possible.

### 3.3 Detection of parent-universe gravitational signatures

If gravity propagates through the Cartasis membrane bidirectionally, sufficiently long-wavelength gravitational waves might carry information about the parent’s mass distribution.

**Tests:** Pulsar timing arrays (NANOGrav, etc.) probe nanohertz gravitational waves. Detection of unexpected features at very low frequencies might be parent signatures.

## Tier 4: Theoretical / computational tests

These are not direct observations but consistency checks computable from the framework.

### 4.1 Filter efficiency yields correct dark matter ratio

Compute filter efficiency from Einstein-Cartan Lagrangian. Predict dark-matter-to-baryon ratio. Compare to observed ~5:1.

### 4.2 Bounce dynamics yield correct CMB spectrum

Compute primordial perturbation spectrum from torsion-driven bounce. Compare to observed CMB acoustic peaks.

### 4.3 Universe size distribution peaks near our parameters

Simulate supraverse population. Show that observer-supporting universes cluster near our observed mass, expansion rate, age.

### 4.4 Asymmetry magnitude from OG + filter compounding

Predict our observed baryon-to-photon ratio $\eta \sim 6 \times 10^{-10}$ from OG fluctuation statistics + filter modulation across lineage depth.

### 4.5 Hubble tension resolution

Show that bounce-modified early-universe dynamics produces $H_0$ consistent with both CMB and local measurements.

## What would falsify SCT?

The framework can be falsified by:

1. **No preferred axis in galaxy rotations at higher precision.** If Shamir’s effect goes away with larger samples, parent-rotation imprint is disfavored.
1. **Dark matter particle detection.** If a fundamental dark matter particle is detected (WIMP, axion, etc.), the geometric interpretation fails.
1. **Dark energy proven constant.** If DESI and successors confirm $w = -1$ at higher precision, dark-energy-as-parent-accretion fails.
1. **CMB spectrum showing recombination-specific features inconsistent with Hawking origin.** Detailed analysis of CMB spectral shape might reveal features that only recombination can produce.
1. **Inflation-specific signatures confirmed.** If primordial gravitational waves are detected at inflation-predicted levels, the bounce-as-inflation-substitute is challenged.
1. **CMB anomalies fully explained by foregrounds.** If “axis of evil” and other anomalies are proven to be statistical or systematic artifacts, parent-rotation signatures are absent.
1. **Bounce simulations fail to produce realistic cosmological evolution.** If detailed Einstein-Cartan bounce calculations can’t reproduce CMB, structure formation, etc., the framework is wrong in detail even if the conceptual structure is appealing.

## Priority ordering for tests

For maximum scientific impact at minimum effort, the priority order is:

1. **Tier 4.2** (CMB spectrum from bounce) — computable now, decisive if it works or fails
1. **Tier 4.1** (filter efficiency yields dark matter ratio) — computable now, strong validation if works
1. **Tier 1.1** (Shamir’s effect with larger samples) — observational program already running
1. **Tier 1.3** (DESI dark energy evolution) — observational program already running
1. **Tier 2.3** (primordial gravitational waves from LiteBIRD) — observational program in progress
1. **Tier 4.5** (Hubble tension resolution) — calculational
1. **Tier 3.1** (gravitational decoherence) — experimental, near-future

Items 1, 2, and 6 are calculational work. Items 3, 4, 5, 7 are observational waits. The calculational work could be done by a small group in a few years. The observational work happens on the experiments’ timescales.
